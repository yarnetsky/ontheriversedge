I’m still hoping to go to graduate school next year, but I came to the conclusion I wasn’t looking forward to the four hour round trip drive to Indianapolis every week for two years. We just don’t have a vehicle that’d handle that kind of driving (let alone a driver).

A couple of people had encouraged me to look at distance education. I had looked a couple of programs but they were all at least twice as expensive due to out of state tuition and required site visits (five days during the summer and a weekend each semester was typical).

Well I found a new one last night. Clarion University of Pennsylvania has just started a web-based program oriented toward rural librarianship (that’s interesting in and of itself).

There doesn’t appear to be site visits (save travel and housing costs), but if it does we have friends who live just an hour away.

Also the costs are only $1,500 more per year than Indianapolis and I’d save at least that much by not have to pay for gas and food every week. For some reason, they charge out-of-state distance learning only 102% of regular tuition (just don’t take a class on campus or they’ll smack your bursar’s account one good).

The only downsides so far is each cohort (a group of students who will take the classes together over the two year period) is limited to 25 students. The very first cohort starts next month with the second starting in January 2005. If I can get in that might be just about perfect as far as timing goes.

I sent away for information, we’ll see what it looks like.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
