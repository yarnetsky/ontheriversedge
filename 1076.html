Only in Madison

It’s been a beautiful day today. A good day for bike riding …. and bike buying.

Since last summer we’ve been saying we’d go over to Fizz’z Bike Shop here downtown and get a bike ordered for Darlene. For fun, she added, “I hope I can get a green one.”

Well, I stopped at Fizz’z this evening because my tires were running a few psi low. Low and behold, there was a custom bike the guys there had put together. It was her size and, of course, it was green.

So I dragged her over there and she took it for a spin. She loved it. She picked out a helmet (yes, we wear helmets and so should you!) and a lock for the bike. Finally, I pulled out the checkbook and—wrong checkbook.

“Just take it all home and come back tomorrow and pay for it,” Joey said.

After a short ride around town on her new bike, Darlene wheeled her bike into the pottery. “Only in Madison could I take a bike home without paying for it,” she said beaming.

Mind you, Joey did say something to the effect of “It’s OK, we know where to find you and Jerry.” Ah, that oh so trusting, mafia-style acknowledgement that you live in a small town.

And yes, he does know where to find me. The night before, Joey had given a “bicycle into spring” workshop for the library that turned out really, really good. I had organized the program and lucked into getting really good press coverage to boot—front page story and three photos. For once I ended up on the front page for something other than Historic Board.

Here’s the article: Expert offers advice for safe, fun bicycling and another photo. Yes, that’s me in the background and that’s my bike he’s using in the program.

And yes, Darlene will be at Fizz’z front door at 10 a.m. tomorrow morning. Actually, she’s writing out the check as I finish this.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.
