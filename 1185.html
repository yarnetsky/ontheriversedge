NBC: Birds in Madison's Wal-Mart

Our Madison Wal-Mart was featured tonight on NBC’s Dateline

The news show was doing an expose on “How safe is your grocery store?” and ranked how the various groceries performed in health checks. Wal-mart didn’t too as badly as some in the NBC survey, but our Wal-mart was featured…

Wal-Mart’s biggest problem in its grocery sections seemed to be keeping food at the right temperature. One of its stores had a real strange problem: birds, live ones. In Madison, Indiana, health inspectors told Wal-Mart it had to get rid of the birds flying around the store.

Dateline: What happens if they drop droppings on the food?

Employee: Well about everything in here is prepackaged, you pretty well clean it up — the only problem is where the deli has fresh food, they just have to throw it out.

Dateline didn’t see any the day we were there but an employee assured us,

Employee: There’s always some around somewhere.

Yes, our Wal-Mart does have birds. I haven’t been there for a while (I try to avoid the place—Wal-marts and malls depress the hell out of me) so I don’t know if the problem has been dealt with or not, but they’ve been there since the place opened.

What can you say… it’s a huge store with huge doors.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
