Ah the weekend.

OK, I survived my Thursday. Got a decent night’s sleep and took another nap this afternoon. Darlene is a siren of the afternoon nap…

Now I’m trying to get a website redesigned so the baker can update her weekly specials on her own. I’m hoping designing a set of blogs will do the trick.

What I’m reading: “The Sands of Time” by Michael Hoeye. “A Hermux Tantamoq Adventure™.”

This is a great children’s mystery-adventure series set in a mythical world of mice. Very English and literate for a kids book.

Hermux is a gentle, but brave “everymouse” and watchmaker. His old friend was blind for three year and her sight was saved by Hermux in the previous book (though I don’t remember how). She is an artist and her first exhibit having sight restored is of visions she had when she was blind. But HORRORS- the paintings are of… gasp… CATS!!! Nude ones at that. In the mouse world cats were aweful mythical beasts that are never spoken of in public. However, Hermux ends up in an adventure that aims to prove them all wrong.

Read the first book “Time Stops for No Mouse” first. These are a great workday reads as the chapters are each only 2-3 pages long. As I get a task done, I’ve been allowing myself to read a chapter. I couldn’t get away with that at work, but hey when I moonlight I do it right.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
