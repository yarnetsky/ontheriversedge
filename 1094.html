Yesterday? homework. Today? homework. Tomorrow?

Well, tomorrow is class, but the day after that will be homework too.

I’m coming up on the end of my first summer session and I’m amazed at how much information is being squeezed into the short space. Having taken two 16-week courses and now one six-week course—I’d prefer quarters over semesters and summer sessions. I think the class is going pretty well, though I’m behind on the reading (what else is new).

Unfortunately, Mother Nature is not helping me much here.

We managed to get air conditioning in our building for the first time just as it hit 94.8 her downtown this week. However, I’d love to know what tree, mold or mite is doing me in. I’m having an extreme allergic reaction to something. I’m wanting to tear my eyes out and it feels like I’m breathing hot sands into my lungs.

Some prescription eye drops I had left over from last summer’s allergy attack helped, but Claritin wasn’t doing the trick so I went in to see the doctor today.

He didn’t tell me anything I didn’t already know—I’m joining the thousands who are suffering from the Ohio River Valley Crud. ORVC is a catch-all malady that is suffered at vaying times of year by valley residents. It typifies the valley as a cauldron of allergies and sinus junk.

Mind you, I didn’t like the prescription—my insurance charged me $40 for Zyrtec-D and I have to take corticosteroid hormone junk for my lungs starting in the morning. While I really didn’t like the side effect from the last time I had to take the stuff, I’ll take that over not breathing. It’s indeed like the billboard about asthma, it is kind of like being a fish out of water.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
