Statistical liars

My view of the Social Security debate is whether or not we want the United States to be what the administration calls an “ownership society.”

Based on the proposals I’m hearing, such as the elimination of employer paid health insurance, it’d be more of an “on your own” society where only the rich would have access to any kind of decent health care or retirement. (For the average American, the so-called medical saving accounts they outline would be wiped out by a single hospital stay).

Either way, Paul Krugman in his recent column points out that the administration, in effort to prove its point, is having a little too much fun with the Social Security numbers.

In essence if we take the assumptions used that the stock market would give great returns for retirement and used the same numbers to Social Security, than SS would be just fine.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
