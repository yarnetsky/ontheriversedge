snippets on 4.4.4

The old Chinese fortune cookie curse goes something like “You will lead an interesing life.”

It has been that.

We went to our nephews wedding which was very nice and as we were about to pack, Darlene’s father had a heart attack. We spent three days with family and in the hospital until her dad got out of the coronary care unit. He has more surgery in his future near and far, but he survived the attack with no damage to the heart because they got him to the hospital so quick.

Meanwhile we hoped to have a quiet weekend to catch up, but our friend Pat was in the mood to build and we now have three new sets of shelves in her studio and other sundry improvements to our building.

Meanwhile at work I’ve got a full slate of projects, I’ll need to work on a massive to-do list because I can’t keep it all in my mind. I’m astounded at how far I can fall behind having missed four days of work.

I also learned we were awarded the full amount of the grant I wrote for wireless Internet at the library. We’ll be able to offer it to the public as well as make access easier for staff. We’ll indeed be a hot spot.

On the pottery side, Darlene’s planning a Grand Open House for the end of the month in her studio. She’s cleaning like a madwoman and plans to make pots like crazy as well. There should be some fun stuff coming out of the kilns so stop by if you can.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
