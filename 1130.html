Cincinnati Union Terminal

As I start fall semester this week, Darlene and I decided to flee Madison one last time and went to Cincinnati. We did a little shopping (I finally visited Costco and got some Perry Ellis chinos for $20) and went to the Cincinnati Museum Center at Union Terminal to see an exhibit on space travel.

While I enjoyed the exhibit, I love Union Terminal. This train palace is an art deco dream and I highly recommend a visit. Since 1990 it has been home to the Cincinnati Museum of Natural History, Cincinnati Museum of History and the Cincinnati Children’s Museum as well as an Omnimax theatre.

But the terminal itself would be worth the trip. Check out these photos from RailStatons.org, especially the rotunda.
The glass fronted rotunda is 10-stories tall with a ceiling of silver, yellow and orange bands. Murals and marble circle the perimeter of the lobby.

We closed down the space exhibit and we were just standing in the middle of the rotunda just drinking it all in when we started hearing voices. “Just let it go…” then a whirr and a clunk. The voices and the sounds were hanging in the air as if they were right next to us, but there was not a soul near us.

It finally struck us that there was a coin vortex by the front door. It was at least 20 feet away, but we could hear every word and sound. What amazing acoustics!

Another person told us that you could stand in one corner of the rotunda and hear a quiet voice in the other corner. Here’s a wonderful photo from Gordon Baer that gives you an idea what I’m talking about.

Pretty impressive no?

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
