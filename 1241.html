Two feet under in Buffalo

My mom emailed us last night that it was snowing and sticking in Buffalo. We went to bed thinking… “ha! that’s Buffalo for you.”

What we didn’t realize is how Buffalo it was. An early-autumn cold blast picked up an extraordinary amount of moisture from Lake Erie which is still a toasty 60 degress. The result was a lake-effect thunder-snow that dumped 14- to 24-inches of the white stuff all over Buffalo metro. Yes, there was thunder and lightning.

The result was trees, still leafed out in autumn splendor, were weighed down with snow. Healthy young trees bent to the ground, but many just lost limbs knocking down power lines and damaging homes.

This morning it was reported that 75,000 customers were out of electricity. The AP reported at 6 p.m. that the number since climbed to more than 380,000 homes and businesses without power.

My mom wrote: ” It was gorgeous, with a lead colored sky and glowing trees. But what a mess.” She sent us some photos, here are a few of them… (click on thumbnail for larger image)

It turned out to be the snowiest October day in Buffalo’s 137 years of recorded weather history. What else would you expect in Buffalo on Friday the 13th?

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
