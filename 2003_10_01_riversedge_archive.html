All's Fair in Love and Euchre

Note: If you don't understand euchre, don't even bother :)

Every few weeks we play cards with our friends who own the local movie theatre. The Ratcliff's have two kids with a third on the way. Thus, when they can manage to get a babysitter, it's party night.

Six-handed Euchre is the game of choice or Uno if we can't get six to play. We keep intending to invite others, but never get around to it.

It was an even split on the night, Laura Ratcliff, Josiah and I won the first round. Tony Ratcliff, our friend Caryl and Darlene won the second round on a misdeal.

We were both in the barn and I was halfway through the winning hand when we realized Darlene dealt me an extra card -- it was a sticky deck. A misdeal was called and Josiah dealt Darlene the winning hand and we lost 10-9.

If that was how the whole night went, I'd been pretty growly. However, it really made us even.

In the first game, it was my deal and I lucked out. I got four hearts in my hand with a low heart turned up. I was planning to pick it up, but Darlene called hearts first. How I managed I don't know, but I kept a straight face the whole time and ruined her hand. She thought she won the last card and I slowly tossed down the last heart a second after they started celebrating.

"Five hearts?! Five hearts!? How could I have known you had five hearts?!" Hearing Darlene say that alone was worth a million.

Morning has broken

After several late hours of preparing for a visit by Darlene's sister, we finally crashed and a woke up feeling as broken as morning.

The morning sun comes in our east facing windows first thing so, because of Darlene's light sensitive eyes, we added a second curtain.

We really don't have a good way of hanging these second curtains so I bought ones with loops and hung a loop on either side of the existing curtain that in turn is on a spring-loaded curtain rod.

As a result, if you can visualize this, the center of the second curtain is hanging loose.

Back to the morning sun. It hits us directly and shines through the area where the second curtain is sagging. On the far wall a "grin" suddenly appears like a Cheshire cat. The crinkled first curtain creating the illusion of teeth on the grin.

After noticing this, we've been able to track the time within a few minutes and the season as it has slowly moved right along the west wall.

Unfortunately, the grin is coming later and later. I'm going to hate waking up at 6:30 pretty darn soon.

We're having a "Soup, Stew, Chilli and Brew" festival on Main Street today. We hope to do our sampling before it starts raining this afternoon. Better yet, her sister and her family will let us take them on a hike this morning to get our hunger up for soup, stew, chili and brew.

Made the mistake of opening the mail

Our budget is shot for a while. We just received our property and business tax bills and they came in about twice what we expected.

Indiana restructured its property assessment methods from an archaic "replacement value" system to a modern "market value" system. The change is hitting the historic district hard because old structures used to be valued low due to depreciation. Under the market system, there's higher demand thus a higher value on the property.

We're lucky actually, I believe we were underassessed. Our assessed value climbed only 30 percent. Many properties downtown doubled if not tripled in value. In turn, our taxes climbed from about $930/year to $1250/year. Obviously, that's not bad compared to what some of our friends are facing.

In the process, our tax bills were delayed. In a normal year we'd be able to split this amount in half payable in May and November. Because of the reassessment, the Spring bills never came so now we have to cough up the whole amount in about 30 days.

It's not bad really, it's just that for some stupid reason we were expecting our homestead and mortgage deductions to kick in this year. They don't kick in until *next* year-- thus we owe a *lot* more than we expected.

Now watch a few of my friends write me back asking why I'm complaining about a $1,250 bill when theirs are a lot higher. Hey, what can I say... we were spoiled.

River Bottom Reading

I hosted a prose and poetry reading at the library last night. It was a quiet affair where people simply came up to the microphone and read their work for a few minutes. We had a better turnout than I expected so it made for a nice evening. There are some good writers here.

I read a story I've been trying to write for about a dozen years. I wrote it and have been rewriting it ever since. It still isn't what I hoped it could be, but hearing myself read it aloud helped me identify where some of the holes are. Unfortunately, they range in the dozens.

I believe I was a decent journalistic writer, but being a fiction writer is another ball of wax. You need to be able to hear the voices of the characters and you have to create a story and twists someone will spend their time reading. I was lucky. I had to dig for my information, but everyone else gave me the stories and twists. Counting on my own mind to invent the stuff seems impossible in contrast.

Fall is here
It is really turning beautiful in this rivertown. The leaves are losing their green, albeit reluctantly. The colors are not brilliant, dull golds and browns. Some red and orange to spice things up. Mind you, I don't think we'l be at peak for another week give or take.

Darlene and I took a nice hike at the Clifty Canyon Nature Preserve, a park of the state park here in town. We have a favorite trail because it goes through mini-canyons and past waterfalls. We usually go the same direction every time, but decided to be contrary and go the opposite way.

It is truly amazing how different the same area looks when you go uphill instead of downhill. There is one stretch of the trail that is literally on the cusp of a 20-foot cliff. We had become used to it and stopped thinking about it. Going the other way it suddenly looked ominous. Just awesome.

Biking to work is great, my wheels crunching in a hum across all the fallen leaves. Our parking lot is just covered, rice crispies from one end to the other.

Unfortunately, the beautiful weather won't last. Tomorrow it'll rain and the cool temperatures will return later this week. I think I'll drag Darlene on a walk tonight (not that it will be an effort-she'll probably drag me first).

Oof, I need a bike ride
It's truly amazing how fast a body can fall apart. I have not gone on my daily bike ride since Friday and boy am I feeling it. Everything's gone stiff and sore. I need to get back to it tomorrow.

This sudden chilly weather doesn't help either. At night we're huddled under a pile of blankets and don't want to get up in the morning. I think it's time to quit pretending this is a cabin and plug in our heater tonight.

Darlene and I are also trying to figure out a way of getting each other out of our little worlds. I'm wrapped up at the library and she in her pots. We went swimming all the time during the summer and miss it terribly-it was our exercise, talking and socializing time and we haven't had that since mid-August. This vacation is starting to help, but we're trying to find a way of not having that "together time" end suddenly.

I need to put the chicken on the grill. One of those cheap $13 George Foreman grills was the best buy we've made in a long time--meat tastes great out of that little thing.
