Didn’t your mother tell you not to run up slides?

I’m not sure, I don’t remember. But experience certainly did last night.
Actually, I lucked out as I narrowly avoided breaking my nose or my teeth.

We were at a picnic celebrating a friend leaving his job. I was playing tag with his kids and a few others hanging out at the playground. I went up a slide to chase one of the kids and slammed into the slide’s metal hold bar. I never saw it—of course it was painted the same color as the rest of the slide

I’m amazed it got it on the upper lip. A quarter inch up or down and I’d have been in the emergency room. My upper lip is a little split and definitely fat. But hey, my mustache hides the worst of it.

Mind you, the evening stayed in that mode.

One of their kids did a literal back flip off the merry-go-round learning his first lesson in centrifical force. He got back on, though holding on very tight, a few minutes later.

Later, while playing UNO, the “unjob” celebrant dropped his playing card in his pop. Darlene threw a paper wad at another friend and it landed in her pop (she couldn’t have hit it if she tried).

At least his wife, who’s expecting their third any day now, didn’t go into labor.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
