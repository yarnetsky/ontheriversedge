Podcasts I listen to

I’ve added a podcast category to my links list at right. Every Friday during my drive to Indianapolis for class I listen to the past week’s podcasts. Without my iPod and these programs my trip would be much less bareable.


  • Open Stacks: Friend Greg Schwartz, a librarian at the Louisville Free Public Library, does this podcast about library and information science topics. Every other week he takes the news of note, gives us the background and his personal thoughts. Each of his 13 episodes has had something I found either interesting or though provoking. If libraries are your thing, this is a must listen.

  • Escape Pod: I don’t know how I stumbled upon this new podcast, but it is an absolute blast. According to creator Steve Eley, cutting edge science fiction had been traditionally written in short story form. However, in part due to the dumping of bad work on the market, the format has dropped out of favor. This podcast hopes to bring the format back to life, reading a new short sci-fi story each week. It’s succeeding to my ears.

  • The MacCast: News for Mac Geek by Mac Geeks. Adam reads the Apple news of the day and contributes analysis. Other members of the MacCast community contribute audio comments, reviews and questions. I usually only listen to the news in the opening of the program, but sometimes the other stuff is pretty good too.

  • On the Media: This is a National Public Radio program posted every Friday by WNYC in New York. This is a great program that doesn’t flinch in its efforts to make sense of today’s media world. I find it fascinating both as a former journalist and current political junkie

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
