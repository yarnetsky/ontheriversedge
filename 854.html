A rant about an alleged “rant”

Living in this “city ‘neath the hills” we don’t watch TV. When we moved here we didn’t have money for cable and now we don’t care.

Thus, for nearly a week now, I’ve been reading about how the Iowa “I have a screech” speech was “bizarre” and “damaging” to his campaign.

Finally, I searched for and found a video of Dean’s speech on the net.

Darlene and I watched it together and looked at each other. Our reaction? With raised eyebrows we asked “WHAT’S THE BIG DEAL?”

The video was taken by a person in the 3,000+ crowd. From their perspective the speech is fine. It makes perfect sense.

Dean had lost and he had a ballroom full of disappointed campaign workers. The experiment of asking people from around the country to show up and campaign had essentially failed. The numbers were there, but these folks were young and very inexperienced.

Secondly, you can tell he’s losing his voice. Indeed, the media noted that just about every candidate was hoarse by the end.

Third, the place was a loud! He started talking, but you could hardly hear him. He had to yell to be heard over the crowd.

Watching it from their standpoint, it sounded like a rallying speech. Yes, he was passionate, but not unlike a football coach rallying his freshman squad.

As Judy Dean said, you could see it as maybe a “little silly,” but that’s it.

The reporters who covered the speech from the scene did a good job. But within a day the media fell into a pattern of rather pitiful coverage. I’m embarassed for the reporters who were sucked into this one.
