Just as things were looking up...

My poor Darlene. She’s had a rather rough past year with fatigue, migraines and more. Well we finally got some news that after all the tests she’s been through one finally showed a positive… hypothyroidism. That’s something that’s very treatable so we’re happy.

HOWEVER…

Saturday night she was bitten by a dog in the arm..

The bite itself was not horrible—bad brusing, a good scrape and a shallow puncture wound. Her jacket protected her from the worst of it.

However, unless we can find the dog, she faces a round of painful and very expensive series of rabies shots (first one alone is $1,000). So far we have not found the #^#$%& dog.

She’s spent the last few days walking around looking for the dog and she is absolutely exhausted. She recognized the dog so she is hoping she can find it again.

I’m going to go out and do some searching this evening. In the meantime, the dog catcher finally came off of being sick and fielded calls all day today from people because the paper ran another article in today’s paper as well.

We have two candidates. One everyone knows, but believes to be basically friendly. Darlene will take a look at that dog tomorrow. There’s another that has been reported to terrorize small animals, but we can’t find that dog.

Wish us luck!

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
