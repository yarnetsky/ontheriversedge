"Life, don't talk to me about life..."

...Sometimes I wonder if Marvin the Paranoid Android had it right.

This morning I had a dream I was on the phone arguing with the electric company over a $128,000 bill. “But I’m a librarian I only make xxx dollars a year!” (as if it would do any good explaining that.) They were charging me $60,000 because they were closing the Madison office of all things.

I finally got mad (or madder rather) and hung up. I was panicking because I was running really late for work. I finally got to work and as I walked in my director said she needed to speak to me.

At that moment the phone rang. I woke up and realized I was indeed 30 minutes late for work as I had forgotten to turn on the alarm clock. I listened to the answering machine and it was a bill collector.

I can understand dreaming about being late for work, but should I really be anticipating the call of a bill collector?

That said, life has interceded and I admit I have not felt the urge to blog for the past few weeks. Basically, as Walt Crawford noted: Life Trumps Blogging

Life… not necessarily in this order…

1. My better half hasn’t been feeling well the last few months and we’re trying to figure out how to get her back to her usual self. She’s going on her fourth specialist so far with no diagnosis. It was a bill collector for the hospital that called this morning. They’re helping us try to figure out what we actually owe them as their billing system sucks.

2. Life is hectic at the library and I’m trying to avoid the feeling of drowning because I took off time to be with Darlene (I’m slowly catching up again… I think).

3. Classes started up again. I’m taking “Materials for children and young adults” and “Evaluation of Library Sources and Services.” Because of parts one and two (specifically part one), I’m not quite into the spirit of non-stop studying though the classes are interesting. Nonetheless, my 4.0 will most likely bite the dust this semester. I know that’s not really a bad thing as very, very, very few people ever care or check. Nonetheless, the concept is taking me a little bit of effort to accept.

4. My mom got me this amazing book for my birthday (39 on 2-10) and it is sitting there taunting me to read it, but I really do need to read more of my homework. I’ve read many books already for the kiddie lit class (really good stuff!)

Something will get me writing again. I’m sure George Bush is plotting his next stupid human trick right now just to get me blogging. Then again, some days there is so much stupidity that I give up trying to keep up with it all.

Until then… toodaloo caribou
