Here come the guns again

When my father died, he was a police officer at Miami University. For the most part it was a peaceful job, but I do remember one of his cohorts being faced with a drug-crazed college student weilding a switch-blade. It could have been a lot worse.

With the ban on semi-automatic weapons set to expire on Monday, it is easy to imagine worse.

Obviously, my father owned guns and regularly practiced their use. A few times he had taken me to the shooting range. I’ve shot a few rounds, but it didn’t really interest me.

My dad said the key to guns is respect and safety. You respect their power, you keep them locked up when not in use. With that in mind, I don’t have a problem with gun ownership. If we ever get our building to a state of non-chaos, I could finally display an antique flintlock my father gave me.

But even with the respect for gun ownership, I still have no idea what use a semi-automatic weapon would be to anyone.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
