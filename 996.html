<strong>sigh</strong>

It’s finally coming to an end. I’m feeling optimistic, but I hope Kerry can win by enough of a margin that our country can avoid lawsuit city. I read an allusion that Ohio is already full of lawsuits, though I haven’t read anything specific.

Meanwhile, I’m not sure which is worse, seeing results come in or not seeing results come in.

The exit polls looked very good for Kerry and that is good, but we lost a Democratic governor in Indiana and I may be losing my Democratic congressman.

I’d love to do something to different than to follow the results. But I don’t think I could concentrate on something like watching a movie.

The results are looking discouraging, but yet it’s too early to be discouraged. When only 25% of a state is reporting, it’s too early to panic.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
