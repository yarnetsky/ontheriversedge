Reagan

Everyone’s flag is at half mast for the death of former President Reagan. While I disagreed with much of his politics, I bow my head in respect for the man. May he rest in peace.

Similarly may Nancy find peace that his life is certainly regarded as legendary. More so, I hope she succeeds in energizing the stem cell research that could have helped her husband.

As far as Reagan’s legacy, I see it as mixed. On one hand he presented America as optimistic and able. In this alone, he was pretty amazing. He also was smart in his dealings with the Soviet Union. While many attribute this to a beefed up military, I think it was more likely his interactions with Gorbachev that did the trick.

On the other hand, he handed down a set of foreign policy experts to Bush 46 who landed him in this Iraq mess. It’s little wonder as many were also involved in the Iran-Contra scandal and the atrosities in central America.

He wasn’t most popular president in modern history (he’s in the middle of the pack with Clinton), but he is certainly a legend. His style crystalized a vision in Republicans’ mind of how the presidency should be.

What’s odd is while Kerry gave a very respectful speach about Reagan at the University of Toledo and suspended campaigning until after the state funeral, Bush is using his death as campaign fodder. Check out his campaign website which has literally been transformed.

I find it overdone, but considering how bad of shape Bush is in, you can’t blame him for trying to see if some of Reagan’s shine can rub off on him.

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.
