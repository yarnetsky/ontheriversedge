Oh please, oh please, oh please

The Boston Red Sox are up 6-0 in the top of the second. Oh please win. This would be the sweetest league championship in history. To be down 3-0 and tie it up is astounding, but to win it in seven. Oh my… Then to have it be the Red Sox, even sweeter.

For starters, I absolutely can’t stand the New York Yankees, they always beat my favorite team, the Kansas City Royals. But more than being an “Anybody But the Yankees,” fan I root for Boston because a good chunk of my blood is from the area. My dad grew up across the river in Chelsea and much of his family is still in the Boston area. He was a Splendid Splinter fan in his youth. I’m rooting for the Sox.

This would rank higher for me than the Royals coming back from deficits to beat the BlueJays and Cardinals in 1985. This would rank as the biggest self-destruction of a giant in baseball history. Oh please….

A two-run homer in the first and a grand slam (from a former KC Royal) in the second… Oh please…

    Fireworks in Madison

    We have a small fireworks show each Regatta week on the Ohio River. It’s nothing extravegant, but it’s fun. You can watch the reflections on the river, but we were on our roof a couple blocks away.

    My favorite thing is listening to the explosions reverberate forever off our 400-foot bluffs. This year’s finale was punctuated by dozens of such explosions which made for a very satisfying ending.
