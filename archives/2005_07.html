07.18.05

Bush "shifting" on Plame blame? Try "shafting"...

OK now, on June 10, 2004, President Bush was asked if he would fire anyone who had anything to do with the leaking Valerie Plame-Wilson's name as a covert CIA agent. Bush responded "yes" and his spokeman since repeated the statement to the White House press corps.

How come today, he is suddenly "shifting" his position as USA Today put it?
Now he'll "if someone committed a crime, they will no longer work in my administration."

To me that essentially says he knows someone (or someones) in his administration most definitely messed with Plame, but he doesn't want to do a damn thing about it.

Striking out at Plame because her husband discredited the Bush rally cry to war would be playground petty if she were not with the CIA. However, she was an agent and to me that borders on treason.

Why? Consider this question... if Plame is outted as a covert agent, isn't that in turn going to out anyone who covertly worked with her?

This isn't the only thing about his statement today that drives me bats-- how about the notion that he's known about the allegation for two years and says he "doesn't know all the facts." For that to be true he must have told everyone involved to keep him in the dark so he wouldn't have to testify.

If there's any saving grace its that the White House press corps finally has a string to pull. They suddenly seem to realize they've been lied to and are trying to unravel the mess we're in. Mind you, the odds of that happening are still pretty slim, but you can't blame a cat for dreaming.

How does blogging impact web accessibility?

Right now I'm taking "Resources and Technology for Patrons with Special Needs" and I'm doing research for a 20 page paper (20 pages maximum thank God).

I write this and Darlene, reading over my shoulder, says "Ha! Knowing you, you'll have a hard time keeping it to 20 pages." I'm glad she's optimistic.

Either way, I'm opening this to the wisdom of the masses.

My topic is how does blogging (and content management software in general) impact accessibility of the resulting websites? Is the software itself user-friendly to bloggers (or would-be bloggers) who have sight impairments? What can we do to improve the situation?

While much of designing blogs for accessibility echoes accessible web design in general, there are other things that are unique to blogging software. For example, in order to post comments on some sites, you have to type in the distorted letters seen in a captcha illustration. That'd be impossible for someone using a screen reader.

In the case of blogger.com, you have to type in the captcha before you can even get a blog. Understandable? Yes, as it keeps computers from signing up for millions of blogs. Accessible? No. How can a person who is blind blog when they can't get a blog from Blogger?

That's one example. Right now I'm harvesting articles for my research. If any of you have any ideas on avenues of exploration, etc., let me know...

07.03.05

Organic groceries in Madison!

Madison has joined the big leagues of food. A new grocery open here downtown that offers organic food, fruit, vegatables, meats and cheeses. The variety is wonderful.

Check out Local Harvest Market. They're planning an "e-market" that will open soon so you can get an idea of what they're carrying.

What amazes me is they even have FRESH fish flown in from the coast three times a week. I don't know the last time I've had fresh fish. Also every kind of meat you can imagine-- we can even get bison, rabbit and duck. If you have the opportunity, you should try organic free-range chicken eggs. They really are pretty amazing.

They also have prepared meals. We had beef curry rice for dinner tonight. They also had grilled swordfish with pineapple and mango sauce. They'll even make you sandwiches from fresh baked bread and premium Boar's Head meats from their deli cut to order. I'm told sushi is also coming soon.

I know one of the people who will be working there and she said they all attended workshops to learn about the products they'll be selling. Indeed, she attended a fullday on just wines and cheeses.

While I doubt I could afford to buy all my food there, but I am very much looking forward to sampling the fare, this will be fun-- real food!
