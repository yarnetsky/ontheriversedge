03.30.05

Only in Madison

It's been a beautiful day today. A good day for bike riding .... and bike buying.

Since last summer we've been saying we'd go over to Fizz'z Bike Shop here downtown and get a bike ordered for Darlene. For fun, she added, "I hope I can get a green one."

Well, I stopped at Fizz'z this evening because my tires were running a few psi low. Low and behold, there was a custom bike the guys there had put together. It was her size and, of course, it was green.

So I dragged her over there and she took it for a spin. She loved it. She picked out a helmet (yes, we wear helmets and so should you!) and a lock for the bike. Finally, I pulled out the checkbook and -- wrong checkbook.

"Just take it all home and come back tomorrow and pay for it," Joey said.

After a short ride around town on her new bike, Darlene wheeled her bike into the pottery. "Only in Madison could I take a bike home without paying for it," she said beaming.

Mind you, Joey did say something to the effect of "It's OK, we know where to find you and Jerry." Ah, that oh so trusting, mafia-style acknowledgement that you live in a small town.

And yes, he does know where to find me. The night before, Joey had given a "bicycle into spring" workshop for the library that turned out really, really good. I had organized the program and lucked into getting really good press coverage to boot-- front page story and three photos. For once I ended up on the front page for something other than Historic Board.

Here's the article: Expert offers advice for safe, fun bicycling and another photo. Yes, that's me in the background and that's my bike he's using in the program.

And yes, Darlene will be at Fizz'z front door at 10 a.m. tomorrow morning. Actually, she's writing out the check as I finish this.

03.29.05

I feel so dirty

When I started grad school I figured I'd never again have an opportunity to purchase affordable software so I purchased about everything that made sense. Mind you, I've been so busy I haven't had time to use any of it.

However, tonight I did and so help me, it put Windows XP on my wonderful Apple PowerBook G4.... aaarrrgggghhh... I feel so dirty.

A friend and fellow Mac user brought over some CDs that for some reason could not be read on a Mac. He couldn't read them and I couldn't read them. So for the first time, I used Virtual PC and fired up Windows XP. Low and behold, I could read the discs.

I use Windows at work so that wasn't a big deal, but it took me a good hour to figure out how Virtual PC worked so I find the files and burn him a new disk on the Mac side of things.

Maybe there's a lesson in all this. Maybe it shows that sometimes you have to compromise yourself in order to attain the greater good. Even though I may hate it, I now know I can run Windows on my laptop if it means helping a friend. Is this what it's like to co-sponsor legislation with a Republican? *wink*

The return of Billmon's Whiskey Bar

One of my favorite bloggers is Billmon over at the Whiskey Bar. He had taken a sabatical as the election wound down and we all missed his voice. Well, he's now back and doing something rather interesting.

03.25.05

Boy am I buzzin'

Well, I just finished my mid-term exam in collection development and my body is buzzing. I had forgotten what it's like to come off of an adrenaline high.

I think I did pretty well on it. Our prof drew a graph on the board of what she expected. Along the top was short and long. On the side was airy and dense. Short and airy, as long as it answered the questions, gets a B. Short and long, odds are you end up with a few details in there, gets a B+. Short and dense with details gets an A- and long and dense with details gets an A. I think I'm on the dense side (pun intended).

I'd have to agree with a classmate of mine. This is our only test in the class and it's for 33% of the grade. All the assignments we've done so far this semester only equal 25% of the grade. Too many eggs in one basket there.

The semester is now on the downhill side, we've learned all the basics and adding the details. The key will be getting all the homework done.

As soon as I catch up with myself here, Darlene and I will drive straight to Lima, Ohio, to spend Easter with her family. While Darlene and her sister are filling plastic eggs for the annual Easter egg hunt, I'll go hide in the living room and do homework. But as soon as the kids show up, I'll be in the thick of it.

Happy Easter. To celebrate the day I want to remember what Jesus actually asked us to do in the world-- as a friend of mine said yesterday "Jesus broke the bread and fish and fed the thousands. He didn't sell them." Unfortunately, that seems to be fighting words these days.

Vacation all I ever wanted...

Just finished our five day mini-vacation. Three days of homework and two days of partying. It was a good combo.

Darlene and I celebrated our 15th wedding anniversary down in Louisville, Ky., the romance capital of the world for the weekend of March 19-20, 2005. I honestly don't know if that's true or not, but we certainly had fun.

We spent the night in a suite, took in an art exhibit, slept, slept, slept. Had really, really good fried green tomatoes, fried catfish and crab cakes (Goose Creek Diner on Goose Creek Road), did a little shopping and swam in a small hotel pool inhabited by a kick of soccer players.

Sunday afternoon was glorious. The first really spring-like day that we got to enjoy. We soaked up the sun on Louisville's riverfront. Walking around, playing frisee and watching in-line skaters do crazy stunts on stairs while a friend recorded it all on videotape (he was trying to create a video for his portfolio with hopes of getting into the Savannah College of Art and Design).

After all that getting back into the swing of studying again has been a bear. I'm not succeeding to be honest.

I have an open book midterm exam in my afternoon class on Friday and I thought I'd be OK until I realized I'm going to the Indiana Library Federation annual conference on Thursday....hmmmm..... I'll just have everyone else drive and I'll read through my notes. As long as I have a general outline in my mind and have everything I need at my fingertips, I should be OK. But still, I'm pushing my luck.

What else is new?

Life, the universe and everything

"Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" the movie is supposed to come out in the next month or so. I saw a preview and it looks like it'll be fun, but already I have one major, major problem with it. Ford Prefect does not have an English accent. Oh well...

We finally have our annex separated off on it its own meter and wiring so we had the electric company turn off the meter so the renter can put it in his own name. Well.... they turned off the electric to the building next door and it took them a day to figure out why.

It's not our fault by any stretch. Apparently, the mix up came when the attorneys who owned our building split off the electric. They mixed up the meters at some point then and it wasn't discovered until we worked on ours.

Otter, take 2

I must admit, I really enjoy otters. When I visit the zoo, otters are by and far the most enjoyable animals to observe. The Cinci Zoo has a decent-sized pond habitat for the otters. The one side is a glass wall that allows you to look underwater while they swim around. They also reintroduced the river otter to Jefferson County a couple years ago up at the Big Oaks National Wildlife Refuge.

So when, maybe six months ago, I saw a kids magazine go across the circ desk with otters on them, the silly pun "otter you be reading?" popped into my head.

Now in SLIS528 Collection Development and Management, I have to create a fictional library. I decided to have an otter as the library mascot and put the drawing on the letterhead.

Our most recent assignment is an intellectual freedom statement along with procedures for dealing with book challenges. The creature is also on my "materials reconsideration form." Who knows, either the otter will help defuse an argument over a book (ahh...isn't he cute?) or the person will find the drawing so darn silly that they'll try to take my head off as well as trying to take the book out of the collection.

03.06.05

Overtime and bankruptcy bills defy family values

You gotta give Republicans credit. They are taking full advantage of their majority status to pass legislation to their liking. Yet, the vindictiveness of these bills blows me away.

With two recent bills, the Republicans prove themselves the party of the corporations.

Bill one: Sen. Rick Santorum is sponsoring a bill that will make overtime applicable to people working more than 80 hours in two weeks. If it passes, an employer could jack you around at will. 50 hours one week, 30 the next, 60 the following and 20 after that.

How is a family to figure out child care then? How will they ever attend a student play or parent-teacher conference?

Even if the 40-hour work week survives, overtime is over.

Bill two: The second, more evil bill, will restructure bankruptcy for individuals. In essence they are eliminating the humane elements of bankruptcy law. If you drown in medical bills from an emergency hospitalization? Tough luck, live with the debt. If you are a soldier who goes to Iraq and your salary goes from $60T to $24T and your family falls into bankruptcy? Tough luck, live with it.

This is not "family values" folks. This is paying off the people who put the Republicans in charge.

Review history from 1870 to 1920 (give or take a few years). Corporations basically ruled the country unimpeded and humans were treated like pieces of a machine to be discarded when the parts broke. This may "sound" like a socialist manifesto, but it's not. It was just reality. The industrial revolution was literally built on the backs of the poor and immigrants.

Here are some photos from the era: History Place: Child Labor in America.

I doubt we'll ever return to the era of child labor, but it certainly gives you an idea of what I'm trying to say.

Once again, I encourage you to write to your congressmen and senators.

Here are a few links that explain the bills:

Daily Kos :: The Bankruptcy Bill, Examined

Daily Kos :: Santorum to End 40-Hour Work Week.

03.04.05

My day in Indy

My collection development professor wasn't planning on writing many comments on a draft document we turned in last Friday... that was until she saw an "it's" that should have been an "its." Seeing that the "dam broke loose," she said, and the comments were flowing. Of course, yours truly had to be one of the offenders.

"I'm surprised that you, having been a journalist, would make that error."
"What can I say, I had copy editors."

Between you and me, I'm amazed my writing has been as clean as it has been lately.

It wasn't a beautiful afternoon, but it certainly wasn't the cold, windy Fridays of recent weeks. Thus I went for a walk and, for the first time ever, visited the Indiana State Library and the Indiana Capitol.

Both buildings have been recently renovated and both are absolutely beautiful and do the state proud.

I walked around the capitol extensively peering in at the Indiana Supreme Court and the house and senate chambers. I stood in the office where former Madison Mayor Morris Wooden sat as state auditor. I even visited the governor's office.

I knew the governor wasn't there. Indeed, he was here in Madison while I was there in Indianapolis.

Nonetheless I gave the person at the desk in his office my three cents.

Penny one: The governor comparing the minority Democrats, who had walked out on the legislative session, to terrorists "car-bombing" his "drive toward reform" was pretty darn tacky when our troops are in Iraq suffering from the real "car bombs."

Penny two: I noted that complaining about it was hypocritical when the Republicans had done the same type things to Democratic administrations. As a classmate of mine stated: "same practices, just the sides have changed."

Penny three: I did say that I liked the idea of daylight savings time.

See I can be bi-partisan.
