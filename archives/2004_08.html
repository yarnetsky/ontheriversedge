Games for Change

OK, so watching Kerry's lead slowly disappear causes one to growl and fret. So Bush has managed to avoid talking about the issues for a couple weeks which, again, causes one to growl and fret. So what?

Thinking about it for a couple of days though, I get the feeling that after the convention Bush will peak out at about 50% support and watch it dwindle away.

Bush has to deal with failures across the board and new ones that keep popping up left and right. He won't be able to control the tempo of the debate. His supporters will go "nucular" with attacks against Kerry and while that will keep some of the electorate undecided, it won't improve Bush's standings. Recent polls are proving that.

The key I think is whether Bush can actually run on his record and offer something to a country that has said it distinctly wants change.

None of this is original thinking. Read Daily Kos and you'll come to the same conclusions.

I think fretting is the result of being blue in a red state, you get the feeling living here there isn't much you can do about it all. However, I think stealing an idea from MoveOn will help. A couple in Massachusettes held a "Games for Change" night as a fundraiser.

We can get a bunch of folks who want some change in the country together here in America's most beautiful rivertown and play games to raise money. Everyone can play games and bid on how they'll go (the bids don't have to be even) and all winnings go into a central pot. Playing volleyball. "We'll bet $2 that we'll make the next point." Win or lose the money goes into the kitty. Start with sports and finish with cards.

Maybe we can decide on a destination for the money up front, maybe we'll hold a vote halfway through the evening as to where the money will go. The DNC? MoveOn? ACT? Congressional candidates? a percentage to each?

The bottom line is we'll have a blast, figure out who we can comiserate with and we'll raise some change for change.

Science Friday: The Precautionary Principal

The British website Spiked has an article about the Precautionary Principal that is rather interesting. Basically we're more sorry than safe is we constantly hedge from taking incremental risks to stay safe:

Berry points to the restrictions imposed on DDT - the pesticide used to get rid of malaria-carrying mosquitoes - as another example of how the 'application of precaution' can cause death and disease. In some third world countries where malaria had been all but eradicated over the past 20 years, there have been epidemics of the disease since DDT was restricted. Currently malaria is on the rise in all the tropical regions of the planet; in 2000, it killed more than one million and made 300million seriously ill. 'Campaigners claimed that DDT was bad for the environment; they said that it caused harm to American birds of prey. I'm sorry, but why should people in the third world at risk from malaria care about American birds of prey? Decisions about these things should be based on local needs and on empirical evidence.'

I suspect anyone interested in the stem-cell research debate would find this an interesting article.

Welcome to the chess match

I think people are starting to realize that slime is part of greater Bushco's general methods of operation.

Depending on what kind of attack comes next, Kerry may have partial immunity and be able to pull off a Reagan "there you go again."

However, I think Bush may have won this round by avoiding having to fight the round.

In his Cooper Union speech, Kerry was right about the biggest loss in the Swifty fiasco. Discussion of the issues has come to a halt in this campaign.

Bush has largely been able to avoid intense coverage of increasing poverty, declining health insurance and reports about Abu Graib. He's able to keep the negatives to a minimum as he's about to grab the convention limelight.

The coverage is there, but Kerry can't pounce on it when he's awash in defending against the slime.

Similarly, this is turning into a big chess match. Everyone attacking the Swifties has in turn given Bush the platform to hammer against Moveon, et al.

I don't know if the public is getting enough information from the "so-called liberal media" to know the difference between Moveon and the Swifties. (The swifties have few huge contributors, Moveon has thousands upon thousands of small contributors).

Thus, I think Bush might be getting away with whitewashing all the groups as "shadowy."

Again, the ability to avoid bad coverage. I'll credit him there, but I don't think his luck can last forever.

Here comes the slime

Man it's going to be a long campaign. I need to remind myself I only have to survive another 72 days of this junk.

I knew the slime would start flying eventually. But now that I've seen the Swift Boat Veterans for the Truth ads, I'm sickened nonetheless.

Unlike Bush, I don't believe that independent advertising should be halted. There are viewpoints in the world beyond Bush and Kerry's. Yet those ads should have a measure of truth.

The Swift Boaters obviously have a beef with Kerry and with how they were treated when they returned home from Vietnam.

In interviews the Swift Boat Vets said they felt betrayed by Kerry and his anti-war protests when he returned from Vietnam. The brush Kerry painted on the war, as he described the attrocities that took place in that country, was wide indeed. Rightly or wrongly, the vets felt they were tarred with the same stroke.

If they want to advertise that fact, that is legit. Kerry himself admitted that his protests and testimony before Congress echoed a youthful anger and he wished he had toned down the language. He told the truth, but the result are wounds that, in Larry Thurlow's words, are "still bleeding." This is fact. I feel for these guys, they were honorable men who were trapped in a war gone awry and not of their making.

But to lie about events and take Kerry's words out of context is inexcusable.

The NYTimes, the Washington Post and an editor from the Chicago Tribune, himself was a swift boat captain on the scene, have all refuted the lies. But the Texas GOP moneybags have plenty of money to keep throwing the muck.

I'm not sure I want to know how this will turn out.

(sorry for the lack of links. I've read all this, but I'm tired and don't feel like digging this moment).

A candidate visits

Darlene was downstair wrapping up her pots for the night (she lets them dry as long as she can before wrapping them up) when a couple was intently looking in the window.

She didn't want to be rude, so she invited them in.

Low and behold, it was the Republican candidate for U.S. Senate Marvin Scott. He's a really nice guy and him and his wife likes Darlene's favorite pots.

They wanted to buy one of Darlene's sculptures, but they have an 8-year-old at home who had earlier broken a piece of pottery from Peru. They appear to have settled on a box vase and may return for it tomorrow.

Is Bush on the verge of panic?

While there wasn't a so-called "bounce" from the convention on the national level, there are some pretty amazing trends developing. Indeed, I'm starting to smell blood.

Kerry, while not perfect, has been clearly enjoying himself and attracting astoundingly large crowds during his cross-country tour.

Meanwhile, the Bush campaign has been a whimper and seems to be on the verge of panic. (Though for equal opportunity, they're saying the Kerry campaign is in panic mode).

Take Oregon.

The photos from Kerry's rally in Portland look more like the Simon & Garfunkle Central Park concert than they do a political rally... more than 50,000 people showed up in the Rose City.... 50,000.

In contrast, the Bush campaign hands out tickets to a select citizens who sign pledges to vote for Bush and has police filter out anyone wearing questionable clothing. Lord forbid if a Democrat or Libertarian were to slipping in. He then holds a scripted "question and answer" session.

Choices of campaign stops is also interesting. Both Kerry and Bush are visiting swing states, but Kerry is visiting Republican cities.

The electoral college is looking very bad for Bush right now -- 327-211 Kerry.

Obviously, swing states such as Ohio, Missouri and Minnesota can swing, but Bush is having trouble holding a lead in states that should be solid Red by now-- we're talking North Carolina, Virginia, Tennessee, Arkansas and McCain's Arizona. In Florida, Kerry holds a 47-41 lead.

Bush's "America's turning a corner" stump speech was pulled because jobs numbers took a surprising downward turn and we'll end up with about 1,000 dead Americans in Iraq by time the GOP convention comes around. Meanwhile, we still haven't heard word one of his second term agenda other than allusions to eliminating the income tax and war with Iran.

The result is that we're watching slime fly everywhere. They're trying to slime Kerry's well documented war record (as opposed to Bush who still can't prove he wasn't AWOL) and they're pulling a "Fox" by taking Kerry's words out of context for slime material.

All signs of panic.

Mind you, he still has his convention. While his State of the Union fell flat on its face (do you still hear about Mars?), he may come up with something. There are also in unpredictables. So who knows...

Kerry still needs to fight like he's behind, but I wouldn't be surprised if this is the start of something bigger. Much bigger.

08.06.04

A wonderful week

Our building was once a hotel in the 1870s-80s. For three days it seemed to be a hotel again as Darlene's sister and 7 of her 8 kids came to visit for three days.

It was a blast. They all had a turn on Darlene's pottery wheel. We took them to see Harry Potter at our 1930s movie theatre here in town. Spent a day swimming at our WPA pool.

Yesterday we took them to the Louisville Zoo. Their new butterfly exhibit is pretty impressive. I love the blue morphos. "Thousands of flights daily" through Sept. 30.

Finally we took them canoeing at a nearby lake today before they headed home.

Two of gang accidentally tipped their canoe. They both shifted the same direction at the same time. ooops.

Seeing them go over I entered "protective uncle" mode and went to the rescue. Actually, I amazed myself by remembering how to right a flooded canoe in deep water from a single canoe. I read about it years ago and had never done it before, but it worked.

They were laughing and having fun. In a way I wish I had let them enjoy it a little more. Instead I ordered them around a bit so I could get them out of the water and save the canoe.

Everyone seemed to have a fun vacation either way. It was great to see them. They're growing so fast.

Finally, I got a new wireless router. Boy the thing was a royal pain to set up. It's why I hate PCs. Apple routers are simple in comparison, but they're between models for another few weeks so I bought a cheapy to hold me over. In the end I gave up and called tech support. But at least I'm up and running again.

Update: Thinking back over the mini-vacation with the kids, one of my favorite times was during the credits of "Harry Potter."

Our friend was the manager that night and she told us we could go up on stage and play in the light. So I went up with all the kids and we all shadow boxed, danced and generally made fools of ourselves. Never in my life has that opportunity presented itself and boy did we take advantage of it.
